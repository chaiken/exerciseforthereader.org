Great Indie Labels

Check out these outstanding indie record labels, where authentic and original music is celebrated and promoted:

  • Absolutely Kosher, especially the high lonesome sound of Virginia Dare and the Mountain Goats' thinking person's pop
  • Alula, offering Gerardo Núñez and Habib Koite
  • Arhoolie, presenter of Clifton Chenier, Flaco Jiménez and the "Sacred Steel" series of pedal-steel and lap-steel recordings
  • Calvin Johnson's K Records, featuring Sarah Dougher, the Gossip, Modest Mouse and Built to Spill
  • Kill Rock Stars, proud sponsors of Sleater-Kinney, The John Doe Thing and the everlasting Billy Childish
  • Matador, includes Pavement and Jon Spencer Blues Explosion (recommend turning off Java first)
  • Oriente, with the mysterious but swinging Kroke (CDs available by mail-order)
  • Peter Gabriel's Real World, offering Maryam Mursal and Hassan Hakmoun
  • Rounder Records, home to Astor Piazzolla, Chris Ardoin and Double-Clutchin, and most of the reggae artists you've ever heard of
  • Rykodisc, offering Cubanismo and the much-lamented Morphine
  • Sugar Free, home of Wheat
  • Triloka, featuring Jai Uttal's Pagan Love Orchestra and the incredible Baly Othmani and Steve Shehan
  • Artemis Records, home to the Pretenders and Steve Earle

And don't forget to have a listen at Epitonic, the web's best source of streaming mp3 playlists, with music far superior to mp3.com. Epitonic has full songs from many of these artists.

I originally heard a lot of this music on KFJC and on KALW's Tangents program. KFJC has a streaming mp3 netcast.

There's also an authoritative list of indie record labels available.
