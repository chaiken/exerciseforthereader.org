X-Ray Diffraction Data on Recent Corvallis InSe/GaSe Films

Alison Chaiken

The films were grown by Bob Bicknell-Tassius. X-ray diffraction data were taken by Paul Bacon. The theta offset for these theta-2theta spectra was chosen to maximize the InSe peaks, which had the effect of minimizing the Si (111) peak at 28.442°. Paul Bacon indicates that (except as noted) all films are 100 nm InSe/10 nm GaSe/miscut Si (111).

Experiments on polycrystalline films (and to a lesser extent, on the MBE-grown films) indicate that In-rich films have better transport properties than Se-rich ones. Taking into consideration peak widths, XRD therefore indicates that the best films grown in 2003 are 020303-2, 020303-4, 020303-3 and 011603-3, approximately in that order. SEM images confirm that 011603-3 has a smooth surface suitable for laser marking. None of the recent films has x-ray peaks as narrow as 042902-3.

  • 030403 x-ray diffraction spectra compared to 042902-2. -2 and -4 have well-resolved InSe and GaSe peaks, just like 042902-2, although -2's InSe peak is broader. -1 appears quite interdiffused, while -6 and -3 look somewhat interdiffused. -5 looks like it has no GaSe.

  • X-ray diffraction scans of the MBE's greatest hits films. 042902-3 and 052802-2 show strong peaks for both InSe and GaSe. The 092502-1 and 092502-3 films do not show GaSe peaks but do have a peak for the conducting orthorhombic In4Se3 phase. The presence of this phase indicates an In-rich stoichiometry. LBNL's photoluminescence data on 042902-3 showed In4Se3 despite the lack of a distinct peak in XRD. The highest EBIC gain has been observed in pieces of 092502-1 that were specially annealed by John Chen.

    The rocking curves for these films show that only 042902-3 has its planes aligned perpendicular to the film normal. The 092502-3 and 052802-2 films have their planes aligned perpendicular to the Si (111) zone axis instead. It's not clear what's going on with 092502-1 since the Si axis is nominally set to +4 ° in this experiment. The 042902-2 rocking curve has a FWHM of 0.41°, while 092502-1 has a FWHM of 0.72°.

  • X-ray scans from run 011403. All six films contain the gamma-In2Se3 phase whose presence indicates a Se-rich stoichiometry. The GaSe peak is not observed. Note the broadness of the InSe peak compared to 042902-3. A shoulder on the h-InSe (004) peak may be an indication of a ternary InGaSe phase. For some InSe films grown on GaSe single crystals, these peaks were strong and well-resolved. A simple calculation shows that most of the unidentified peaks match a hypothetical material with c = 16.47Å. By comparison, h-InSe has c = 16.640Å and h-GaSe has c = 15.907Å. The 042902-3 data have been divided by 10 for ease of comparison.

    Rocking curves for these films have a FWHM of about 1.6°.

  • The run 011603 films have various secondary phases. Several of the films show distinct GaSe peaks, especially 011603-6. 011603-2, 11603-3 and 011603-4 all show the ternary shoulder. 011603-4 and 011603-6 both show some In4Se3 while 011603-2 and 011603-7 show a In2Se3. 011603-3 and 011603-5 have a weak peak in-between the In4Se3 and In2Se3 peaks that could belong to either (or neither) phase. 011603-1 appears to be single-phase, but the Si peak could be obscuring a secondary-phase peak. The 042902-3 data have been divided by 10 for ease of comparison.

    Rocking curves FWHMs for these films vary from 1.2° for -6 to 1.6° for -2.

  • The run 012903 films have In2Se3 as a secondary phase, meaning that they are Se-rich. It's not clear whether the -7 film's slightly lower-angle peak is also In2Se3. The GaSe peak here is only a poorly resolved bump. There is no sign of the ternary shoulder. Note how broad the peaks of the 012902 films are compared to 042902-3, whose data have been reduced by a factor of 10.

  • The films of run 020303 differ greatly from each other. Unlike the previous 2003 runs, all the films have well-resolved GaSe peaks. -2, -3, -4 and -5 all have In4Se3 peaks, while -6 has two In2Se3 peaks. -1 has a peak at 27.2° which does not match any of the likely phases well. The width of the XRD peaks for these films is narrower than for previous 2003 depositions. Note that the 042902-3 data is not scaled here.

    Rocking curves show that the 020303 films have their planes aligned more perpendicular to the Si (111) zone axis than to the substrate normal. Their appears to be a trend in alignment versus InSe film thickness, but caution is required since all wafers may not have the same miscut, or may be rotated slightly differently. The 020303-4 has the best rocking curve width, about 0.69° FWHM.

  • The run 0210 films appear to be completely single-phase. However, the GaSe peak is poorly resolved and the InSe peaks are rather broad.

    Rocking curve FWHMs are about 1.1°. The planes are aligned normal to the Si zone axis rather than to the surface normal.
