Abstract

Stage 1 potassium--mercury graphite intercalation compounds with markedly different superconducting transition temperatures, Tc, and transition widths, delta Tc, are found to have uniformly high Tc and narrow transitions after exposure to hydrogen. These hydrogen--doping experiments suggest a schematic model for the density of states in stage 1 KHg--GIC's.
