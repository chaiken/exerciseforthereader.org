Account of Our 10-Day Trip around Brazil

Wolfgang and I went for a 10-day long trip around Brazil following the conference I attended at the International Center for Condensed Matter Physics at the University of Brasilia. Our travel arrangements were made by Robert at Brazil Reservation System, (800) 544-5503.

Rio de Janeiro

For the first several days we hung out in Rio on the beach. I knew Wolfgang would be jet-lagged, so I figured suntanning would be a relatively untaxing introduction to South America for him. We stayed at the Leme Palace Hotel, on the beach at the northern end of Copacabana. The location, location, location of this hotel was excellent, but it was a bit rundown; we got bitten by fleas and everything needed a coat of paint. The view from the seaside balcony was outstanding: Cariocas playing futbol, having bonfires, surfing, and just in general raging. The hotel room cost $119 per night. The first day we walked along the Copacabana beach and drank beer. Vendors sell beer on the beach: cool! If I were Al Gore, I'd run on a "A Beer on Every Beach" campaign in 2000 (but that's why I'm not Al Gore). The beach is remarkably beautiful, even compared to the Northern California coast. It's hard to believe that the granite promontories all around are real having been to Las Vegas and Disneyland. We live in the San Francisco, CA Bay Area, and I must say that Rio is the only more scenic city in the world. The lagoons, cliffs, hills, promontories, beaches and views are more stunning than SF. We had a great time watching the locals playing futbol and tableless tennis on the beach. (No frisbees!) We got horribly sunburned, something which negatively affected the rest of our trip.

The second day we walked fully clad all the way south to Ipanema. Remarkably Ipanema is even more beautiful than Copacabana. From Ipanema you have an excellent view of Corcovado, where the giant Christ statue is. To walk to Ipanema from Copacabana you travel through Arpoador, a major surfing beach with excellent views to both the north and the south. There is a fenced-in stray cat sanctuary in Arpoador; the rest of the city is obviously ruled by stray dogs. Also in Ipanema we went to a crafts fair where really attractive leather bean-bag chairs were on sale for $100 each without filling. Put off by the price (and effort of hauling 'em back), we didn't buy any. If you know a stateside source, please do email me. The first rule of travel in Brazil is that all roads lead to Sao Paulo. You can go anywhere you want as long as you fly to Sao Paulo first. Given that we had a Brazil Airpass and traveled around Brazil a bit, we saw a lot of the Sao Paulo airport. Travel in Brazil is limited by the fact that there are typically only a few flights per day between most of the major cities, so your itinerary on a short trip will be strongly impacted by available flight times.

My overall reaction to the trip was that it was a lot of fun, but more expensive than I'd expected. Despite the current strength of the dollar, it cost me $21 to do my own laundry at a Brazilian laundromat and the best rate we got for a cab to the hotel in Rio was $38. I'd do this trip again in a second, but I'd take along more money the next time! Between the two of us, we took along about $900 in cash, and we brought back less than $100. This is despite the fact that all our lodging and air transportation were prepaid. There can be no doubt that we would have had more fun on this trip had we taken along $2000 and been less worried about running out of money.
